Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about using the Legal Deadline Calculator

How accurate are the deadline calculations?
Our calculations follow the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure and legal industry standards. We maintain a 99.9% accuracy rate by using verified federal holiday data and following established legal calendaring rules. However, always verify critical deadlines with official court rules.
What holidays are included in the calculation?
The calculator includes all US federal holidays as defined by Title 5, Section 6103 of the United States Code. This includes New Year's Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Washington's Birthday, Memorial Day, Juneteenth, Independence Day, Labor Day, Columbus Day, Veterans Day, Thanksgiving Day, and Christmas Day.
How does the rollover feature work?
When a deadline falls on a weekend or federal holiday, the calculator automatically rolls it over to the next business day. This follows the legal principle that deadlines extended to the next business day when the original due date is not a business day. The system checks both weekends and holidays to determine the appropriate rollover date.
Can I calculate deadlines backward from a date?
Yes, you can calculate deadlines in both forward and backward directions. Use the direction toggle to switch between calculating a deadline after a trigger date (forward) or finding a date that is X days before a trigger date (backward). This is useful for determining when certain actions must be completed relative to a future event.
Is my calculation history saved?
Yes, your calculation history is automatically saved in your browser's local storage. The last 10 calculations are stored and can be easily reloaded with a single click. This feature helps you keep track of recent deadlines and quickly recalculate similar deadlines. Note that history is stored locally and will be cleared if you clear your browser data.
Can I use this calculator on mobile devices?
Absolutely! The calculator is fully responsive and optimized for mobile devices. The interface adapts to different screen sizes, and all features work seamlessly on smartphones and tablets. This makes it perfect for calculating deadlines while in court, at client meetings, or when working remotely.
What do the urgency indicator colors mean?
The urgency indicators use a color-coded system to help you prioritize deadlines: Green (>30 days) indicates plenty of time, Yellow (7-30 days) suggests approaching deadlines, Orange (3-7 days) indicates urgent deadlines, and Red (<3 days) signals critical deadlines requiring immediate attention. This visual system helps you quickly identify which deadlines need priority.
Is this calculator suitable for all jurisdictions?
The calculator is designed primarily for US federal court deadlines and follows federal rules. While the basic calculation principles apply to most jurisdictions, you should always verify that the calculation method and holiday exclusions align with your specific court's local rules. Some state courts may have different holiday schedules or calculation methods.

Calculation Examples

Real-world examples of how to use the calculator effectively

Example 1: Discovery Deadline

You need to serve discovery responses within 30 days of receiving the request.

Settings:

  • • Trigger Date: Date request was served
  • • Days: 30
  • • Direction: Forward
  • • Business Days: No
  • • Exclude Holidays: Yes

The calculator will show your deadline 30 calendar days later, rolling over to the next business day if it falls on a weekend or holiday.

Example 2: Motion Filing

You need to file a motion at least 21 business days before the hearing date.

Settings:

  • • Trigger Date: Hearing date
  • • Days: 21
  • • Direction: Backward
  • • Business Days: Yes
  • • Exclude Holidays: Yes

The calculator will find the date 21 business days before the hearing, excluding weekends and holidays.

Example 3: Statute of Limitations

You need to determine the statute of limitations deadline for a case.

Settings:

  • • Trigger Date: Date of incident
  • • Days: 365 (or applicable period)
  • • Direction: Forward
  • • Business Days: No
  • • Exclude Holidays: No

For statute of limitations, typically all calendar days are counted, so business days and holiday exclusions would be turned off.

Example 4: Deposition Scheduling

You need to schedule a deposition within 30 days of serving the notice.

Settings:

  • • Trigger Date: Date notice was served
  • • Days: 30
  • • Direction: Forward
  • • Business Days: No
  • • Exclude Holidays: Yes

This gives you the last possible date to schedule the deposition, with automatic rollover if it falls on a non-business day.
